# Deep-Ocean Physics and Chemistry

Status date: April 19, 2026.

## Physics Core

- Pressure:
  - In seawater, pressure increases by about 1 atmosphere every ~10 m (NOAA reference).
- Temperature:
  - Most deep ocean remains cold, often near 0-4 C depending on basin and circulation.
- Light regime:
  - Deep ocean generally starts where sunlight dwindles (~200 m and below).
- Flow dynamics:
  - Internal waves, boundary currents, topographic steering over ridges/canyons.

## Chemistry Core

- Dissolved oxygen and oxygen minimum zones:
  - Strongly shape habitability and metabolism.
- Carbon chemistry:
  - CO2 uptake and acidification affect carbonate-forming organisms, including deep corals.
- Vent and seep chemistry:
  - Sulfide, methane, hydrogen, and metal-rich fluids fuel chemosynthetic food webs.
- Particle flux:
  - Organic export (marine snow) links surface productivity to deep benthic food supply.

## Depth-Linked Constraint Table (working)

- 0-200 m:
  - High light, rapid biogeochemical cycling.
- 200-1,000 m:
  - Low light, strong vertical migrants, key remineralization zone.
- 1,000-4,000 m:
  - High pressure, cold and dark, low energy flux.
- 4,000-6,000 m:
  - Abyssal conditions, very low food input in many regions.
- 6,000+ m:
  - Hadal trench systems, extreme pressure and specialized biota.
