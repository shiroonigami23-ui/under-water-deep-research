# Ongoing Biology Studies (2025-2026)

Status date: April 19, 2026.

## Current High-Relevance Study Streams

### 1) Global biodiversity discovery acceleration

- Ocean Census reported 800+ new marine species and planned additional species discovery workshops and expeditions through 2025.
- Early 2026 updates include additional Coral Sea discoveries, indicating continued high discovery rate.

Why it matters:
- Baseline taxonomy is still incomplete for deep systems.
- Conservation and regulation lag when species are undescribed.

### 2) Telepresence and deep ROV exploration campaigns

- NOAA Ocean Exploration 2025 operations and annual report show major mapping and biological observation expansion, including high-biodiversity communities and potential undescribed species.

Why it matters:
- Better mapping and specimen collection improve habitat modeling and biodiversity prediction.

### 3) High-resolution visual ecology datasets

- MBARI 2025 deep-sea highlight releases continue to expand annotated visual records for rare taxa and behavior observations.

Why it matters:
- Behavior and species ID in deep systems depend heavily on long-term video archives.

## 2025-2026 Governance Context Affecting Biology Research

- ISA negotiations (30th session, July 30, 2025 communication) continued mining code work and intersessional groups.
- High Seas Treaty reached threshold for entry into force on January 17, 2026 (as reported on Wikipedia; verify against UN treaty status pages before formal policy use).

Why it matters:
- Access, impact assessments, and conservation regimes shape what biology research is funded and where.
