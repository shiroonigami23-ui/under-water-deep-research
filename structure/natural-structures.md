# Natural Deep-Water Structures

Status date: April 19, 2026.

## Primary Natural Structure Classes

- Oceanic trenches:
  - Deepest plate-boundary depressions; core hadal environments.
- Seamounts and guyots:
  - Biodiversity islands in deep pelagic contexts.
- Submarine canyons:
  - Sediment and organic matter transport corridors from shelf to deep sea.
- Hydrothermal vent fields:
  - Volcanic/tectonic fluid systems with chemosynthetic ecosystems.
- Cold seeps and brine pools:
  - Chemically distinct benthic habitats with specialized biota.

## Active Research Themes

- Endemism and connectivity across seamount chains.
- Canyon-driven transport and benthic food pulses.
- Vent biogeography and geochemistry-biota coupling.
- Disturbance and resilience in trench and abyssal ecosystems.

## Priority Mapping Need

- Integrate bathymetry, geology, and biodiversity observations into shared GIS layers.
- Separate known, candidate, and unconfirmed hotspot structures.
