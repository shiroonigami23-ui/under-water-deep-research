# Deep-Water Types and Geographies

Status date: April 19, 2026.

This file classifies deep-water environments for your study workflow.

## Type A: Open-Ocean Depth Zones

- Epipelagic: 0-200 m
- Mesopelagic: 200-1,000 m
- Bathypelagic: 1,000-4,000 m
- Abyssopelagic: 4,000-6,000 m
- Hadopelagic/Hadal systems: 6,000+ m

Use when studying broad water-column ecology and global gradients.

## Type B: Volcanic-Adjacent Deep Water (your "volcanic close ocean")

Typical settings:
- Mid-ocean ridge and back-arc vent provinces
- Volcanic arcs with deep slopes and hydrothermal fields
- Seamount chains with volcanic substrate and vent/seep interactions

Why distinct:
- High geochemical flux
- Chemosynthetic ecosystems
- Strong coupling between tectonics and biology

## Type C: Continental Margin Cascade (Land -> Water -> Deep)

Typical sequence:
- Land basin / river input
- Continental shelf
- Continental slope
- Submarine canyon / rise
- Abyssal plain or trench

Why distinct:
- Strong sediment and nutrient transfer pathway
- Important for canyon biology and deep food supply

## Type D: Semi-Enclosed or Land-Water-Land Basins

Your requested case: "below land -> water -> land and below that deep water"

Examples include:
- Fjord systems (silled deep basins)
- Inland/semi-enclosed seas
- Deep troughs within marginal seas

Why distinct:
- Restricted exchange with open ocean
- Strong stratification potential
- Often unique oxygen/chemistry dynamics in deep layers

## Type E: Trench-Arc Systems

- Extreme-depth convergent boundaries
- Hadal habitats plus nearby volcanic arc influence

Why distinct:
- Maximum pressure environments
- High endemism and extreme adaptation research potential

## Type F: Chemically Extreme Deep Pockets

- Brine pools
- Anoxic deep basins
- Methane seep clusters

Why distinct:
- Sharp chemical boundaries
- Specialized microbial and metazoan assemblages

## Recommended Study Matrix

Track each case with four tags:
- `bio`: dominant taxa and adaptations
- `structure`: geomorphology and substrate
- `physics`: pressure, temperature, mixing
- `chem`: oxygen, pH, sulfide/methane, alkalinity
